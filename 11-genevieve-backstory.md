# Genevieve → Lacey Lovely, Backstory (Facilitator)

**De Worsti's first wife was Genevieve.** She is now **Lacey Lovely**, hospital director, after total facial reconstruction and legal name change. **Officer Ray is an old friend and protector** who knows who she used to be. Only a handful of people in the building can connect the identities tonight.

---

## Who Genevieve was

Genevieve grew up in the regional youth sanctuary alongside **Dr. Curie**, arm in arm in the orphanage photo (E2-D). She was bright, guarded, and allergic to authority figures who promised safety and delivered control.

Before De Worsti, she had already learned not to rely on rescue. Ray became one of the few old connections she trusted, and three years ago he finally understood what De Worsti had turned her life into.

As a young woman she married **De Worsti** before his empire fully hardened into what Lesbian City underground papers would later call a **loan shark and enforcement network**, protection rackets, predatory lending, debt collectors like **Uvula Sachs**, and violence dressed up as family loyalty.

---

## Life under the crime lord

De Worsti did not merely cheat or shout. He **ran Genevieve's life as property.**

| Abuse | Detail (facilitator truth) |
|-------|---------------------------|
| **Isolation** | Moved her between apartments; cut her off from sanctuary friends; monitored calls |
| **Financial erasure** | Every account in his name; she had pocket money, not autonomy |
| **Physical violence** | Beatings when she questioned deals or flinched from his "business guests"; old bruises like the one on De Worsti's wrist in prologue, she wore them first |
| **Sexual coercion** | Implied in newspaper clipping (redacted); Alfie can testify she "stopped flinching in public and started flinching alone" |
| **Punishment escalations** | After she helped herself to cash from a locked ledger, De Worsti burned **Curie's family home** to teach her what running costs |
| **Hunt** | When she vanished with stolen millions, De Worsti hired P.I.s, searched facial reconstruction clinics, and used hospital access whenever medicine gave him cover |

Genevieve did not stumble into a new life. She **bought one with blood money and surgery**, new face, new name (**Lacey Lovely**), flagship hospital post in Lesbian City where De Worsti would never think to look.

## Why Ray acted tonight

For years Lacey believed the new face and title kept her safe. **Ray** found her three years ago, took the security chief post, and swore revenge on Alex.

Then **subtle warnings** turned into a pattern:

| Warning sign | What Lacey / Ray read it as |
|--------------|-------------------------------|
| **Uvula Sachs** on VIP perimeter | Enforcer muscle, De Worsti's network, not random street noise |
| Collection faces in parking / loading | Same Indigo District posture Sachs trained |
| **Vance** reporting odd database sweeps | Wife hunt accelerating toward admin tier |
| VIP intake with sparse records + bought priority | De Worsti's signature, even before names matched |
| Storm week chaos | Cover for people who already lived in chaos |

**Ray** confirmed the worst when De Worsti's **real surgical booking** landed: VIP arterial repair, compromised vessel, **legitimate medical need**. De Worsti was not faking illness to sneak in. He was dying slowly enough to shop for a stage.

His play was psychological: book at **her** hospital, let department heads fuss over him, assume she would freeze. **Homes** refused the theater (meritocrat, insulted by wallet ethics). **Curie** refused the theater (sanctuary bond, saw the trap). Ray decided Alex would not walk out.

Ray did not chase him through the wing. **Sachs** was on perimeter, cameras were still mostly live, and a VIP financier killed anywhere except the table would lead straight to the badge that escorted him in. Alex's real case was the trap Alex built for himself: one window where he'd be sedated, staff could be chemically frozen, lights could die on Ray's schedule, and a violent scalpel wound would read as rage in the theater—not murder by security. Alex wanted to own Lacey's OR; Ray finished him on the table Alex chose, dressed the death as medical chaos, and walked out still running the room.

**Facilitator line:** De Worsti's surgery was real. His arrogance was real. Lacey's fear was real. Ray's cut was still murder.
