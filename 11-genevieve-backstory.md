# Genevieve → Lacey Lovely, Backstory (Facilitator)

**Alex Austin's first wife was Genevieve.** She is now **Lacey Lovely**, hospital director, after total facial reconstruction and legal name change. Only a handful of people in the building can connect the two identities tonight.

---

## Who Genevieve was

Genevieve grew up in the regional youth sanctuary alongside **Dr. Curie**, arm in arm in the orphanage photo (E2-D). She was bright, guarded, and allergic to authority figures who promised safety and delivered control.

As a young woman she married **Alex Austin** before his empire fully hardened into what Lesbian City underground papers would later call a **loan shark and enforcement network**, protection rackets, predatory lending, debt collectors like **Swastika Sachs**, and violence dressed up as family loyalty.

---

## Life under the crime lord

Alex did not merely cheat or shout. He **ran Genevieve's life as property.**

| Abuse | Detail (facilitator truth) |
|-------|---------------------------|
| **Isolation** | Moved her between apartments; cut her off from sanctuary friends; monitored calls |
| **Financial erasure** | Every account in his name; she had pocket money, not autonomy |
| **Physical violence** | Beatings when she questioned deals or flinched from his "business guests"; old bruises like the one on Alex's wrist in prologue, she wore them first |
| **Sexual coercion** | Implied in newspaper clipping (redacted); Alfie can testify she "stopped flinching in public and started flinching alone" |
| **Punishment escalations** | After she helped herself to cash from a locked ledger, Alex burned **Curie's family home** to teach her what running costs |
| **Hunt** | When she vanished with stolen millions, Alex hired P.I.s, searched facial reconstruction clinics, and used hospital access whenever medicine gave him cover |

Genevieve did not stumble into a new life. She **bought one with blood money and surgery**, new face, new name (**Lacey Lovely**), flagship hospital post in Lesbian City where Alex would never think to look.

## The 48-hour plan (why tonight)

For years Lacey believed the new face and title kept her safe. Then **subtle warnings** turned into a pattern:

| Warning sign | What Lacey / Curie read it as |
|--------------|-------------------------------|
| **Swastika Sachs** on VIP perimeter | Enforcer muscle, Alex's network, not random street noise |
| Collection faces in parking / loading | Same Indigo District posture Sachs trained |
| **Vance** reporting odd database sweeps | Wife hunt accelerating toward admin tier |
| VIP intake with sparse records + bought priority | Alex's signature, even before names matched |
| Storm week chaos | Cover for people who already lived in chaos |

**Curie** confirmed the worst when Alex's **real surgical booking** landed: VIP arterial repair, compromised vessel, **legitimate medical need**. Alex was not faking illness to sneak in. He was dying slowly enough to shop for a stage.

His play was psychological: book at **her** hospital, let department heads fuss over him, assume she would freeze. **Homes** refused the theater (meritocrat, insulted by wallet ethics). **Curie** refused the theater (sanctuary bond, saw the trap). Lacey told Curie they had **about two days**. The murder plan was improvised in that window, vault, vial, blackout, incision, frame, log scrub. Desperate, not elegant.

**Facilitator line:** Alex's surgery was real. His arrogance was real. Lacey's fear was real. The kill was still murder.
