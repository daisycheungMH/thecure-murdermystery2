# Genevieve → Lacey Lovely, Backstory (Facilitator)

**De Worsti's first wife was Genevieve De Worsti, born Genevieve Roper in 1957.** She is now **Lacey Lovely**, hospital administrator, after total facial reconstruction and legal name change in 1980. **Officer Ray is her mother and protector** who knows who she used to be. Only a handful of people in the building can connect the identities tonight.

---

## Who Genevieve was

Genevieve grew up with **Ray** and **Ajax**. **Ray Roper** had her in **1957** at age twenty-two. When Ray and Ajax split in **1966**, nine year old Genevieve was placed with **Ajax**, who was cruel to her and called it discipline. Ray spent the next three years in police training and began searching for her through LCPD channels in **1969**. Ajax owed **Alex De Worsti** money and fell short on payments. In **1976** he traded nineteen year old Genevieve into marriage with Alex: half hoping a power broker would give her a better life, half wanting her out of his house. **Ajax vanished from the record after signing.** Genevieve was young, guarded, and trapped.

**Ray** hunted her using **LCPD resources** through the 1970s. Internal affairs **discharged** her in **August 1977**. Alex sent **Uvula Sachs** and other enforcers to burn **Ray's residence** on **13 September 1977** as a warning. **Two dead:** Ray's **mother Gemma Roper** and **sister Junie Roper**. Ray was not home. She carries survivor guilt and swore revenge on Alex from that day forward.

In the mid **1980s**, after she escaped and rebuilt her identity, Lacey studied public health and met **Dr. Curie**, then deep in surgical training. They became close friends. Curie learned the full truth about Alex later and carried that anger into tonight.

As a young wife Genevieve married **Alex De Worsti** before his empire fully hardened into what Lesbian City underground papers would later tag **Purple Reign**: a loan shark and enforcement network, protection rackets, predatory lending, debt collectors like **Uvula Sachs**, and violence dressed up as family loyalty.

## Life under the crime lord

De Worsti did not merely cheat or shout. He **ran Genevieve's life as property.**

| Abuse | Detail (facilitator truth) |
|-------|---------------------------|
| **Isolation** | Moved her between apartments; cut her off from Ray and university friends; monitored calls |
| **Financial erasure** | Every account in his name; she had pocket money but zero autonomy |
| **Physical violence** | Beatings when she questioned deals or flinched from his "business guests"; old bruises like the one on De Worsti's wrist in prologue, she wore them first |
| **Sexual coercion** | Implied in newspaper clipping (redacted); Alfie can testify she "stopped flinching in public and started flinching alone" |
| **Warning fire** | After Ray hunted her on the force, Alex ordered the **residential arson** that killed Ray's mother and Junie (E4-H) |
| **Hunt** | When she vanished with stolen millions, De Worsti hired P.I.s, searched facial reconstruction clinics, and used hospital access whenever medicine gave him cover |

Genevieve did not stumble into a new life. After **four years of marriage, 1976 to 1980**, she fled with stolen ledger money at age twenty-three. She **bought a new life with blood money and surgery**, new face, new name (**Lacey Lovely**), and eventually a flagship hospital post in Lesbian City where De Worsti would never think to look.

---

## Why Ray acted tonight

For years Lacey believed the new face and title kept her safe. **Ray** found her again in **1988**, when Ray was fifty-three and Lacey was thirty-one, took the security chief post soon after, and swore revenge on Alex. The fire, the lost years, and the reunion made tonight inevitable once Alex walked back in.

Then **subtle warnings** turned into a pattern:

| Warning sign | What Lacey / Ray read it as |
|--------------|-------------------------------|
| **Uvula Sachs** on VIP perimeter | De Worsti's network; same enforcer who burned Ray's home |
| Collection faces in parking / loading | Same Indigo District posture Sachs trained |
| **Vance** reporting odd database sweeps | Wife hunt accelerating toward admin tier |
| VIP intake with sparse records + bought priority | De Worsti's signature, even before names matched |
| Storm week chaos | Cover for people who already lived in chaos |

**Ray** confirmed the worst when De Worsti's **real surgical booking** landed: VIP arterial repair, compromised vessel, **legitimate medical need**. De Worsti was not faking illness to sneak in. He was dying slowly enough to shop for a stage.

His play was psychological: book at **her** hospital, let department heads fuss over him, assume she would freeze. **Homes** refused the theater (meritocrat, insulted by wallet ethics). **Curie** refused the theater (university bond, saw the trap). Ray decided Alex would not walk out. **Lacey** heard the booking and aligned with her on logistics: administrator codes, wing lock on cue, admin cover two floors up while the record cleared her. She never entered the OR.

Ray did not chase him through the wing. **Sachs** was on perimeter, cameras were still mostly live, and a VIP financier killed anywhere except the table would lead straight to the badge that escorted him in. Alex's real case was the trap Alex built for himself: one window where he'd be sedated, staff could be chemically frozen, lights could die on Lacey's override, and a violent improvised wound would read as rage in the theater while Homes held theatre steel. Security-chief murder would have been obvious anywhere else. Alex wanted to own Lacey's OR; Ray finished him on the table Alex chose, dressed the death as medical chaos, and walked out still running the room.

**Facilitator line:** De Worsti's surgery was real. His arrogance was real. Lacey's fear was real. Lacey opened the night from admin. Ray's cut was still murder.
