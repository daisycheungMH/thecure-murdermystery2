# Evidence prop reference (text + object photo)

**Host only.** One entry per clue: episode/evidence ID, short description, full player text, and a **deletable** reference photo of the physical object.

**How to use**

- Delete any `<!-- PHOTO START -->` … `<!-- PHOTO END -->` block to drop that image from this doc.
- Photos are **real web images** (Wikimedia Commons, CC licensed). Not AI generated. Most are phone snapshots or casual documentation shots.
- Swap a photo: replace the `![...](...)` URL or save a local copy under `player-pack/assets/evidence-photos/` and point the path there.
- Player handout copy and drop timing: [15-evidence-materials.md](15-evidence-materials.md), [04-evidence-index.md](04-evidence-index.md).

**Optional local download:** run `python player-pack/download-evidence-photos.py` to cache images into `player-pack/assets/evidence-photos/`.

### E1-A · Blood pattern card

**Description:** OR floor diagram plus annotated note on blood found on Dr. Homes's surgical wear. Two distinct floor behaviors near the operating table, plus cast-off on the primary surgeon's gown.

**Text:**

> **ST. SAPHICCA'S PROGRESSIVE CARE, VIP OR TWO, FLOOR SKETCH (HOST COPY)**  
> Officer Ray, LC-4471  
>
> **Zone A (pre blackout):** Controlled drip, south of table. Elevated arterial spray. Small. Precise.  
> **Zone B (post blackout):** Splash and smear, east tile. Fast movement in the dark, or the body shifted.  
> **Gown (Dr. Homes):** Chest and left forearm flecking from table spray. Heavier cast-off on right shoulder and gloves from floor-level work. Operative bleed, then a second burst at center.  
> **Note:** Two phases. Doesn't fit one surgeon frozen in place.  
> **Clinical footnote (subtle):** One carotid cut at center would read continuous on floor and gown. The gap suggests work **in the dark after staff were down**, apart from any rage stroke at the table.

### E4-H · Newspaper clipping, Ray residence fire

**Description:** Older article about arson at the Roper family duplex. Names **Gemma Roper**, **Junie Roper**, and **Ray Roper**; Ray's discharge in **August 1977**, her hunt for Genevieve, and police suspicion of a **financier-linked Indigo syndicate**.

**Text:**

> **LESBIAN CITY HERALD** · Metro · 14 September 1977
>
> **Mother, Daughter Burn**
>
> Municipal fire officials said on Monday that an overnight blaze at a residential duplex in the Indigo District had killed two people and that investigators had opened a parallel inquiry into a **lending syndicate** active in the same neighborhood. Internal police memos later referred to its principal by the street title **Purple Reign**; the press never printed the legal name.
>
> Victims were identified as **Gemma Roper, 71**, a retired textile worker who had lived at the address for two decades, and her daughter **Junie Roper, 35**, a part-time nursing student who shared the unit with her mother. Neither was believed to have ties to the loan operations that dominate the district.
>
> **Ray Roper**, Gemma's elder daughter and a former LCPD officer, was not home when the fire started on the night of **13 September 1977**. Internal affairs confirmed she had been **discharged in August 1977** for unauthorized use of department resources while searching for **Genevieve Roper**, a missing young woman believed connected to a financier's household in the same district.
>
> Investigators said the blaze was **inconsistent with faulty wiring**. Neighbors reported luxury sedans leaving the block before engines arrived. Enforcers including **Uvula Sachs**, linked to the syndicate's collection network, were seen near the property that night, according to police sources.
>
> "We are treating this as a deliberate act and following every line that connects the residence to outstanding collection matters," a fire investigation spokesperson said.
>
> No charges were filed at the time. A syndicate spokesperson called the deaths "a tragic coincidence unrelated to collection matters."
>
> *(Players connect Gemma + Junie Roper, Ray's discharge hunt for Genevieve, E4-G ledger fire line, and tonight's VIP patient.)*
