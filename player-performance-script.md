# The Cure, Player Performance Script

**St. Saphicca's Progressive Care, VIP Wing.** You are a suspect, a witness, or both. This booklet has lines to deliver and scenes to play. It does **not** have the full mystery solution.

---

## HOW TO PLAY

**Read your character dossier first.** Everything about who you are, what you want, and what you must hide is in your private dossier. This script only has shared scenes and your spoken lines.

**Stay in character.** Talk like your person. React to the storm, the lockdown, and each other. Argue. Flirt. Lie. Protect your secrets until it costs you something to keep them.

**Only read the act you are in.** When the facilitator says you are in Act 1, read through Act 1 and **stop**. Do not read Act 2 until the facilitator opens it. Same for Acts 3 and 4. Spoilers ruin the night for everyone.

**Lines under your character name** are yours when that scene is reached. Optional lines are yours to use or skip. Parentheses are scene cues. Stage directions in *italics* are movement only.

**NARRATOR** blocks are read by the facilitator. Listen, then play.

**Between scenes** you investigate the wing, trade information, examine evidence, and accuse each other. Officer Ray, Officer Marlowe "Marz", and Dr. Curie lead interviews, but no one is a spectator.

**Rules:**

- The VIP wing is sealed. No one leaves until LCPD arrives (end of the night).
- Do not physically grab other players or props unless the facilitator clears it.
- Evidence cards are real in the fiction. You may lie about what you saw. You may not invent evidence the facilitator has not given the room.
- Death in the OR is fixed canon. You cannot rewind the murder.
- Your dossier **✓** and **✗** rules still apply.

**Personas:** Play big and fun. Your dossier and [14-actor-personas.md](14-actor-personas.md) have how to play your character.

**Officer Diamond:** Security is stretched across the hospital, so Diamond joins from main campus after the code and reaches the lounge after Ray's first briefing. See [player-dossiers/11-officer-diamond.md](player-dossiers/11-officer-diamond.md).

### NARRATOR: THE TRUTH

> Long before the operating table, before the storm, before the red emergency lights, there was a locked house and a woman called Genevieve learning how to become smaller than a man's rage.
>
> In De Worsti's world, the Indigo syndicate called him Purple Reign. Debt was religion, cruelty was discipline, and possession was love. Ever since he was young, he was always great at breaking people down to what they could pay, and when money was gone, he took blood.
>
> Genevieve ran when she finally could. She paid for a new face with fear and cash and sleepless years. She took a new name, Lacey Lovely, and built an administrative life out of rules, schedules, and controlled doors. When Alex booked her hospital, she knew Ray had a plan. She opened what needed opening, locked the wing on cue, and stayed upstairs where the record would clear her. She never entered the theater.
>
> It was not enough for De Worsti.
>
> He found the thread, clinic photos, reconstruction records, a scar above the left ear, and followed it back to St. Saphicca's. He arrived as a patient and predator, smiling through intake, already planning what he would reclaim.
>
> Officer Ray had spent years on LCPD chasing Indigo loan sharks. She was really Genevieve's mother, named after her mother and sister. When Ray and Ajax split, nine year old Genevieve went to Ajax's household, then into marriage with Alex when Ajax sold his debts with her hand. Ray searched through department channels until internal affairs discharged her in August 1977.
>
> On 13 September, Sachs and Alex's men burned Ray's home in the Indigo District. Gemma Roper and Junie Roper died in the fire. Ray was not home. She swore revenge that night and never let the search go.
>
> Genevieve vanished in 1980 with stolen ledger money and a rebuilt face. Ray found her again in 1988 as Lacey Lovely, running St. Saphicca's VIP wing. Ray took the security chief post to stay close. In public they barely knew each other. When De Worsti booked real surgery in Lacey's hospital, Ray accelerated a plan the room would mistake for medical conspiracy. Lacey coordinated from admin: codes, wing lock, crisis posture two floors up.
>
> Vault access opened the path. Potassium chloride prepared the room. Backup power failed at exactly the right minute. Sedation lagged the witnesses. In darkness, one prepared hand drove a razor through his throat in a violent, almost crazed stroke. Then panic, blood on Homes's gown, and Dr. Homes framed by timing and terror while Curie became the red herring.
>
> By sunrise, the script was cracked open. And for once in Lesbian City, the rich did not buy the ending.
>
> De Worsti died the way he made others live, trapped, cornered, at the mercy of people he thought he owned.  
> Justice arrived late, it arrived incomplete. But it arrived.

**END OF NIGHT.**
