# PLAYER DOSSIER, CONFIDENTIAL

CHARACTER NAME: Dr. Curie  
AGE: 38  
OCCUPATION: Chief of Surgery and medical liaison on De Worsti's death

## 1. PUBLIC PERSONA & TRAITS

**Look**
You wear impeccable scrubs or a blazer, keep steady hands, and make warm eye contact. You give off prince charming energy without trying.

**Likes**

- scalpel work
- competent partners
- pottery

**Dislikes**

- Homes's ego and your rivalry with him
- De Worsti
- cruelty

**Vibe**
You are still very charming: an idealist, generous, seriously professional, a kind-hearted social butterfly who is genuinely friends with almost everyone in the wing. Your warmth has an **annoying charisma** to it: you are the room's angel's advocate, always finding the bright side and assuming the best in people because, most of the time, people rise to it. That optimism is **not an act**. You mean every kind word, but in a murder wing it can read suspicious anyway, and you cannot help it. **Dr. Homes** is your professional rival; he finds your niceness obscene and you find his ego exhausting. Deep trauma lives under the charm, but you never show it.

## 2. BACKSTORY & VICTIM

**Connection**
De Worsti was tonight's VIP patient, and the man who burned your family home and hunted Genevieve.

**History**

- You grew up with Genevieve (now Lacey Lovely) at a regional youth sanctuary, failed to stop her marriage to De Worsti and what came after, and after she fled De Worsti torched your family home until everyone inside died.
- You wanted Alex dead. You did not kill him. Officer Ray runs this investigation, and the room will mistake your motive and your skill for guilt.

**Motive**
You wanted De Worsti dead after the fire and everything he did to Genevieve. The room can name that motive in one breath.

**Opportunity**
You carry surgery-chief OR clearance and everyone knows your skill. You were in the eastern VIP corridor during the blackout, close enough to look guilty. Your keycard log names you at the OR reader during the kill window, but you were in the corridor and someone lifted the badge without you noticing.

**Earlier tonight (roughly 8–11 p.m.)**
You worked liaison checkpoints and the VIP corridor: consult handoffs, warm contact with half the wing, pottery small talk with anyone who stopped you. You refused De Worsti's theater on principle and stayed out of the OR until the crisis pulled everyone toward the code.

## 3. ALIBI (11:42 p.m.)

**Where**
You were in the eastern VIP corridor and at liaison checkpoints, not in the OR at the moment of the kill.

**Backup**
Staff saw you moving in the corridor, and Ray trusts your partnership on the investigation. You can feel that he respects you, and you assume the best in your friends, so you do not suspect him.

**Hide (minor)**
You considered violence when De Worsti's booking landed. You did not act. If pressed, say you were coordinating triage from the corridor. You never realised your OR keycard left your person that night until the evidence names it. Your corridor is a blur of warm contact: hugs, handshakes, someone steadying your elbow while you read a chart. You have too many friends to say which one lifted it, and you will not throw suspicion at people you love on a guess.

## 4. SECRET OBJECTIVE

**Goal**
Protect Lacey, survive the red herring heat, and help the room find the truth without confessing to a crime you did not commit.

**Plan**

- Build a warm partnership with Ray. Light flirtation keeps you at the table, but never openly block leads that point away from Lacey. You trust him because you assume good faith in your friends; do not play paranoid.
- When access logs surface, be honestly baffled: your card, your OR clearance, but you were in the corridor. You cannot name which friend took it from among the many who crowd you every shift. That contradiction is real to you, not a performance. The room finds the truth through opportunity (a torn log page, a mainframe pull, a witness who lived long enough), not because you turned on anyone.
- Offer real medical insight. Tell Ray a lead seems unlikely but is still worth running.
- When the room chases your sanctuary bond or surgical skill, stay calm. Your rivalry with Homes is real: needle him when he deserves it, praise him when the medicine is sound. You are not sweetening your image on purpose; your genuine niceness just makes you look guiltier, and Homes's irritation makes him look guiltier. Let both happen.

## 5. WHEN EVIDENCE SHIFTS (play the turn, do not lecture)

- **Early:** Blood on the floor (two phases, not one frozen surgeon) and staff down before the cut both point **away** from a simple OR rage story. Say so clinically when those cards surface.
- **Mid:** Toxicology clears Jetski and Pepper; power and vault logs show **admin/security tier**, not surgery chief at the console. Your motive stays hot; your **mechanism** cools if players read carefully.
- **Late:** Torn keycard page plus raw mainframe name your badge, not your body. Be genuinely shocked. Corridor witnesses back you. Wound report (E4-E) is too crazed for your usual theatre work.
- **Never:** Confess to a cut you did not make. Let opportunity without presence do the work.

## ⚠️ RULES

- **✓** Use charm lines, praise, dry flirt, medical jargon, and angel's-advocate upside when others pile on a suspect.
- **✗** Do not confess to the murder. Do not break with Lacey. Do not openly block Ray when he digs into Lacey. Do not **perform** innocence through niceness. You are actually this nice, and that is the problem.
