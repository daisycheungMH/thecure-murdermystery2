# Actor Personas, Performance Guide

**Plot beats unchanged.** These are exaggerated, fun lenses for how to *play* each character, bigger energy, same story.

---

## Dr. Curie, Prince Charming (villain under the hood)

**Surface:** Shoulders back, warm eye contact, remembers names, offers coats and coffee. Classic leading man charm, the room *wants* to be on his side.

**Under the hood:** Deeply traumatized (Genevieve, the fire, the pact). **Never let it show**, only the prince. When stressed, get *more* charming, not less.

**Physical:** Open posture; unhurried hands; smile that lands like a gift.  
**Voice:** Low, warm, complete sentences. Never shouts, cuts with politeness.  
**Tell:** When lying, he gets *extra* helpful.

> *"Officer, I'd flirt back, but we have a corpse."* (smile, pivot, devastating competence).

**Only Homes** sees through the performance. Lean into that annoyance.

---

## Officer Ray, Bruiser with a soft center

**Surface:** Square jaw energy, boots loud, notebook like a weapon. First impression: *will arrest the weather.*

**Soft center:** Protective of innocents, secretly romantic about partnership, genuinely wants justice. Curie flusteres him, he hates that he likes it.

**Physical:** Takes space; points with chin; softens voice only for Curie or when admitting debt shame.  
**Voice:** Gravel, short sentences, words a normal person would use. Occasional accidental tenderness he covers with gruffness.  
**Tell:** Rubs back of neck when embarrassed.

> *"Don't tell me how to ask questions."* (said like he already took the note).

---

## Officer Diamond, Southern belle (brutally honest)

**Surface:** Posture like church on Sunday, voice like sweet tea, nails wet from a fresh coat; you paint them when you think, and it looks like fidgeting because it is.

**Under the hood:** Junior to Ray but not junior in brains, so you will say the outlandish thing out loud because someone ought to.

**Physical:** Pin curls or neat bun; uniform pressed; lipstick careful; notebook with little stars in the margin.  
**Voice:** Drawl, polite, simple words, then a theory that clears the room.  
**Tell:** Paints a nail, blows on it, delivers catastrophe like weather.

> *"Honey, I'm not saying the director cut him. I'm saying if I were her, I'd have been upstairs making sure nobody thought I could."*

**Melody beat:** She asks where your outfit is from, and you answer **kindly** because you are not cruel.

**Vance beat:** You go soft when he panics and do not tease him for liking Melody, even though your heart is your own problem.

---

## Nurse Pepper, Sage (sentimental + logical)

**Surface:** Still water and hospital monk energy; you speak like you've read the chart *and* the room.

**Sentimental:** Grief for Alex is real and messy beneath the calm, while your crush on Homes stays quiet and not performative.

**Logical:** When she switches to data mode, it's surgical, timestamps, vitals, no fluff. The throat clear is her **only** undignified habit *once she's composed again.*

**OR scene (Ep 1):** Let the terror **shatter** the sage mask, shaking hands, broken voice, can't finish sentences. She recomposes later; this is the first crack.

**Physical:** Economical movement; clipboard as sceptre.  
**Voice:** Measured; drops one precise line then silence.  
**Tell:** *Ahem* when emotions get too loud in the room.

---

## Dr. Homes, Authoritative dick (for a good cause)

**Surface:** God complex with receipts. Condescending because he *is* the best surgeon in the wing, and saving lives matters more than your feelings.

**Not cartoon evil:** Believes in the medicine. Hates Curie's charm because it's **unearned** in his religion of merit.

**Physical:** Chin up; invades personal space; gestures with instruments or pens like scalpels.  
**Voice:** Lecturing timbre; sighs before other people speak.  
**Tell:** Says *"Doctor"* correcting his own title when insulted.

> *"Could we investigate the murder instead of auditioning?"* (peak Homes).

---

## Lacey Lovely, Girl next door damsel (corporate)

**Surface:** Pretty, approachable, *harmless*, cardigan energy in a director's badge. People underestimate her because she looks like someone who needs protecting.

**Corporate side:** Ice underneath. Liability language, boardroom smile, crisis voice on speakerphone.

**Play the gap:** Damsel in the light; mastermind in the pause before she answers.

**Physical:** Soft angles; stillness; smile that doesn't reach eyes when cornered.  
**Voice:** Warm alto for public; flat executive for threats.  
**Ep 4 denial:** Sincere sounding, *"I only wanted to run"*, delivered like a woman who's practiced being believed.

---

## CD Vance, Genki IT girl

**Surface:** Nerdy, hyperactive, **cutesy**, stickers on laptop, anime keychain energy, talks fast when nervous (always nervous).

**Terror reads as:** Speed, babble, oversharing technical detail nobody asked for. *"Okay okay okay so the logs are like, "* then accidentally confesses.

**Physical:** Bounces on heels; fidgets with cable ties; big eyes.  
**Voice:** Upbeat, climbs in pitch under stress; uses gamer/dev slang wrong on purpose.  
**Tell:** Nervous laugh after every dangerous fact.

**Melody crush:** Goes full genki disaster around her, helpful to a fault.

> Sachs threatens her → she still says *"eep"* and does the query.

---

## Swastika Sachs, Loyal knight (second in command, German)

**Surface:** Enforcer as **chivalric code**, brutal, but principled in her own twisted way. Alex was the liege; she was the sword. Now the liege is dead and the realm owes answers.

**Not dumb muscle:** Speaks in duty, debt, honor among thieves. Mocking her name is attacking her crest.

**German accent (mandatory):** Lines in the script are pre-accented. When you improvise, swap **D→T, B→P, G→K, V→F** (good→goot, rob→rop, dog→dok, love→luff).

**Physical:** Still as armor; economy of violence; wire cutters like a reliquary.  
**Voice:** Formal cruelty; German consonants; unexpected courtesy when someone "gets it."  
**Tell:** Calls people *"sir"* or *"lady"* right before threatening them.

> *"You ton't moralize at me, sir. You were on the clock."*

**Vance:** Treats as squire, coerced, but *hers*.

---

## Melody, Valley gossip bimbo

**Surface:** Valley cadence, hair touch, *"like literally"*, plays ditzy so people talk in front of her.

**Under the hood:** Sharp as a nail file. Bimbo is **camouflage**. Never drop the act until it pays.

**Physical:** Lean in; wide eyes of fake surprise; phone that isn't there.  
**Voice:** Upspeak; drawl; gasps on cue, then one clean factual sentence that ends careers.  
**Tell:** *"Oh my god, wait, "* before dropping a nuclear detail.

---

## Alfie Austin, Shifty nervous coward

**Surface:** Wannabe operator, borrowed confidence, delivery driver cosplay, knife he doesn't know how to hold.

**Truth:** Folds under pressure; tells the truth in **hedges** because he's scared of everyone, Alex's memory, Lacey, Ray, Sachs.

**Physical:** Can't stand still; wrings hands; looks at exits.  
**Voice:** Speeds up when lying; voice cracks on Genevieve's name; apologetic mid sentence.  
**Tell:** *"I mean, probably, I could be wrong, "* before the most accurate thing anyone says all night.

**Ep 4:** Protective of Lacey/Genevieve because family guilt, not courage.

---

## Dr. Jetski, Frazzled surfer bro (supporting tone)

**Surface:** Sweating anesthesiologist who peaked in the wrong specialty. Sun bleached, hospital ID crooked, every panic is physical, wipe brow, shaky hands.

**Under the hood:** Actually sharp. Knows the chemistry cold. The surfer bro layer is real, not a bit, you trained coastal, you still talk like the beach when the adrenaline hits.

**Play:** Comic relief pressure valve; rage when nickname mocked. Surfer speech is **occasional**, not constant. One *dude* or *bro* per scene lands harder than a whole monologue in slang.  
**Voice:** Whiny competence with surf cadence bleeding through; knows the pharmacology, hates the drama.  
**Tell:** Stress makes you sound more like a surfer, not less.

> *"Dude, that's not drift, that's a wipeout in a vial."* (then immediately correct yourself like you hate that you said it).
