# Evidence design (host + future edits)

Rules for new or revised clues. Player handouts: [15-evidence-materials.md](15-evidence-materials.md). Drop map: [04-evidence-index.md](04-evidence-index.md).

---

## Core rules

1. **Obtainable:** Every clue exists as a card, printout, or prop from a **search location**. No NPC memory gate (*"I think there was a document"* without that document in the pile).
2. **Show, don't tell:** Player-facing copy = what they see or hold. Exact words on a note. No conclusions. No **Subtle read** on handouts (host description or index absolution tables only).
3. **Digital OK:** Screenshots, terminal exports, printer output, still frames. Avoid **log-shaped** repetition (timestamp dumps, query exports that all read the same).
4. **Players solve:** NPCs alibi, lie, panic. They do not walk the room through what a clue *means*.
5. **Mystery diversity:** Rotate formats (scene object, photo, handwritten scrap, invoice, redacted fax, ledger, press clip, label mismatch). Benchmark: **E2-G** red poncho + razor, **E1-A** splatter, **E2-D** snapshot.
6. **Tone:** Procedural and cold for institutional clues. Not cartoon (no red X on photos, no scrawled *FIND HER*).
7. **Prose:** Follow [.cursor/rules/prose-style.mdc](.cursor/rules/prose-style.mdc).

## Player card vs host

| Layer | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Evidence card / prop** | Physical or digital artifact description. What is written on it. |
| **Host description** | Inference, pairs, drop timing, absolution. |
| **NPCs** | Cannot be the only source for a clue. |

## Ray arc (mechanism)

Early: Homes frame, Lacey/Curie heat. **E2-I** (-R note), **E2-G** red security poncho, **E2-J** access chart (Security Chief cannot open OR Two). Mid: **E3-A** CCTV (empty OR Two hallway 9pm–midnight; Curie on eastern liaison cam during blackout). Late: **E4-A** Curie badge lost and found that day + **E4-C** keycard swipe during blackout + **E3-A** video conflict (tampered or ghost timestamps). OR approach was one person (Ray), not a downstairs pair.

## Anti-patterns

- NPC-only clues
- Cards that state the conclusion
- Log-shaped read-aloud blocks
- Duplicate shapes in one episode (second newspaper, second dossier, second query log)
- Medical or IT jargon on player-facing copy when a simpler object works

## Cover-test

Hide host notes. Can the room get every handout from **search alone** and still argue productively?
